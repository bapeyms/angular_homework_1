The Real Pioneer: Phil "Steam" Shaw

This hobby isn't just a meme; it has a real founder. The sport was invented by Phil Shaw from Leicester, England, in 1997. Known by his nickname "Steam," Shaw decided to shake up his boring household chores. Coming home from a long day at a knitwear factory, Shaw was faced with a pile of ironing. He preferred the idea of going rock climbing, so he decided to combine the two. He took his board and iron to the cliffs, and a new sport was born. 1999: Shaw went on a world tour to promote the activity. 2002: He organized the first World Extreme Ironing Championships in Germany. Legacy: His book, Extreme Ironing, and various documentaries have inspired thousands of "ironists" worldwide.
